Citizenship through Naturalization

This is the most common way for foreign-born persons to become a U.S. citizen. Applicants must be 18 years old and fall into one of the three categories below:

More on Naturalization
You should file the following: Form N-400, Application for Naturalization.

Citizenship through Acquisition

A child may acquire citizenship automatically if born outside the United States if a parent was a U.S. citizen at the time of the child's birth.

More on Acquisition
You should file the following: Form N-600, Application for Certificate of Citizenship.

Citizenship through Derivation

When a parent naturalizes, his or her children, under the age of 18 and living with the parent, may derive U.S. citizenship automatically, provided they are also permanent residents.

More on Derivation
